## **1. How to join two or more sentences together**

Two or more sentences can be joined using **〜て(で)**.
### **1) V₁て-form、[V₂て-form、] V₃**

- When mentioning two or more actions that take place in succession, they are listed in their order of occurrence using the **て-form**.  
- The tense of the whole sentence is determined by the tense of the **last verb**.

---
### **2) い-adj (drop い)→ 〜くて**

- おおきい → おおきくて
- ちいさい → ちいさくて
- いい → よくて (exception)

---
### **3) な-adj (no な)→ 〜で**

---
### **[Note]**
- 〜て(で) **cannot be used** to connect two adjective sentences with the same subject if they show **contrasting** descriptions.  
- In that case, **が** is used (Lesson 8-4).

× このへやはせまくて、きれいです。  
〇 このへやはせまいですが、きれいです。  
_This room is small but clean._

## **2. V₁て-form から、V₂**

- In this pattern, **V₂** expresses something done **after** **V₁**.  
- V₁ is often a **precondition** or a preparatory action for V₂.  
- The tense is determined by the **last verb**.
- The subject of **Vて-form から** is marked with **が**.

---
## **3. N₁は N₂が adj** です

This pattern indicates that **N₁** (the topic) has the characteristic: **"N₂ が adj"**.

## **4. Nを V**

With verbs such as **でます** and **おります**, the particle **を** indicates a **starting point**.

# **Lesson 17**

---
## **1. Vない-form**

The form of a verb that attaches to **ない** (e.g., **かか** in **かかない**) is called the **ない-form**. The method depends on the verb group.
### **1) Group I Verbs**

- Change the final **い-column** sound of the ます-form to the **corresponding あ-column** sound.  
- But if the verb ends in **～います, it changes to **わ** instead of あ.

- かきます → かかない
- よみます → よまない
- とります → とらない
- すいます → すわない
- いそぎます → いそがない
- あそびます → あそばない
- まちます → またない
- はなします → はなさない
### **2) Group II Verbs**

The ない-form is the same stem as the ます-form.

- たべます → たべない
- みます → みない
### **3) Group III Verbs**

The ない-form is the same stem as the ます-form.

- べんきょうします → べんきょうしない
- します → しない
- きます → こない

---
## **2. Vない-form ないでください**
_Please don’t do …_  

- Used to ask or tell someone not to do something.  
-  Also used to show consideration by telling someone they don’t have to do something.

---
## **3. Vない-form なければなりません**
_Must do …_  

- This pattern expresses obligation.  
	(Although it looks negative, the meaning is positive.)

---
## **4. Vない-form なくてもいいです**
_Need not do …_  

- Used to say that doing something is not necessary.

---
## **5. Making an object the topic**

- When making **NをV** into the topic, place the noun at the beginning and replace **を** with **は**.

---
## **6. N(time)までに V**

- Indicates a time **by which** an event ends or an action must be completed.

**Note:**  
- **まで** (Lesson 4) indicates the end point of a continuing action.  
- Do not confuse it with **までに**.

# Lesson 18

---
## **1. Dictionary form of verbs**

The dictionary form is the basic form of a verb. How it is formed from the ます-form depends on the verb group.

### **1) Group I Verbs**

Change the final **い-column** sound of the ます-form to the **corresponding う-column** sound.

- かきます → かく    
- いそぎます → いそぐ
- よみます → よむ
- あそびます → あそぶ
- とります → とる
- まちます → まつ
- すいます → すう
- はなします → はなす

### **2) Group II Verbs**

Add **る** to the stem (same stem as ます-form).

- たべます → たべる
- みます → みる
### **3) Group III Verbs**

- します → する
- きます → くる

---
## **2. (N / V-dictionary form + こと) ができます**
_Can do …_  

- できます expresses ability or possibility.  
- The thing that can be done is marked with **が**.

### **1) With nouns**

Use nouns of motion (うんてん, かいもの, スキー, ダンス) or nouns showing ability (にほんご, ピアノ).

### **2) With verbs**

Attach **こと** to the dictionary form to make it a noun phrase, then add **ができます**.

---
## **3. わたしのしゅみは (N / V-dictionary form + こと) です**
_My hobby/interest is …_  
- Using **V-dictionary form + こと** describes the hobby more specifically than a simple noun.

---
## **4. [ V₁-dictionary form / Nの / Quantifier (time period) ] まえに、V₂**

_… before …_

### **1) Verb**
- V₂ happens before V₁.  
- V₁ **always** uses the dictionary form, regardless of V₂’s tense.

### **2) Noun**

- Add **の** after the noun.  
- Nouns expressing motion can also be used.

### **3) Quantifier (time period)**

- Do **not** add **の** after a time period quantifier.

---
## **5. なかなか**

When followed by a negative verb, it means _not easily_ / _not as expected_.

Example:  
① 日本ではなかなか馬を見ることができません。  
You don't see many horses in Japan.
### **Note:** Topic formation

- **が** and **を** → change to **は**    
- Other particles (で, に, へ, から, まで, と) → keep the particle and add **は** (→ では, には, とは, etc.)

---
## **6. ぜひ**

Used to strongly express the speaker’s hope or desire.

## **Lesson 19**

---
### **1. Vた-form**

- Forms of verbs ending in **た** or **だ** are called their **た-form**.  
- A verb’s **た-form** is created by changing the **て** or **で** of its **て-form** to **た** or **だ** respectively.

| て-form | た-form |
| ------ | ------ |
| かいて    | かいた    |
| のんで    | のんだ    |
| たべて    | たべた    |
| きて     | きた     |
| して     | した     |

---
### **2. Vた-form ことがあります**
**have the experience of V-ing**  

- Used to say something that happened in the past as a **particular experience**.

---

### **3. V₁た-formり、V₂た-formりします**
**V₁, V₂, and so on…**  

- This pattern is used to list **a few typical actions** from a longer list.  
- There is **no time order** between the actions.  
- The tense appears at the **end** of the sentence.

**Note:**  
- Do not confuse with **V₁て、V₂て、V₃...** from Lesson 16, which lists **all** actions in sequence.  
- V₁たり、V₂たりします is **non-exhaustive**, so it is not used for everyday routines like waking, eating, sleeping.

---
### **4. 〜なります (become …)**

Indicates a **change of state**.
#### **1) い-adjectives**

Drop **い**, add **く** → **〜く なります**
- さむい → さむく なります

#### **2) な-adjectives**

Add **に** → **〜に なります**
- しずか → しずかに なります

#### **3) Nouns**

Add **に** → **N に なります**
- ３５さい に なります

# Lesson 20

---
## 1. **Polite style and plain style**

The Japanese language has two styles of speech: **polite style** and **plain style**.

### **Polite style vs. Plain style**

| polite style        | plain style      |
| ------------------- | ---------------- |
| あした とうきょうへ いきます。    | あした とうきょうへ いく。   |
| まいにち いそがしいです。       | まいにち いそがしい。      |
| すもうが すきです。          | すもうが すきだ。        |
| ふじさんに のぼりたいです。      | ふじさんに のぼりたい。     |
| どいつへ いった ことが ありません。 | どいつへ いった ことが ない。 |

- The predicates used in polite-style sentences accompanied by **です** or **ます** are called the **polite form**, while the predicates used in plain-style sentences are called the **plain form**.

---

## 2. **Proper use of the polite style or plain style**

### 1. **Conversation**

The polite style is used when speaking to:
- a person one has met for the first time,
- someone of higher status,
- people in a similar age group whom one does not know very well.

The plain style is used when talking to:
- one’s close friends,
- colleagues,
- family members.

Be careful not to use the plain style to the wrong person, as this would be discourteous.

### 2. **Writing**

The polite style is commonly used in **letters**, while the plain style is used in **dissertations, reports, diaries**, and so on.

---
1. **Conversation in the plain style**

Questions in the plain style usually omit the particle **か** from the end, and are pronounced with a **rising intonation**, e.g.  
のむ（↗） or のんだ（↗）

①  
こーひーを のむ？（↗）  
…… うん、のむ。（↘）

2. In noun and な-adj questions, **だ** (the plain form of **です**) is omitted.  
    In an answer in the affirmative, ending the sentence with **だ** could sound a bit harsh, so it is either omitted entirely or replaced by a sentence-ending particle to soften the reply.

3. In the plain style, certain particles are often omitted if the meaning of the sentence is evident from the context.

However, particles like **で、に、から、まで、と** are not omitted because the meaning would be unclear without them.

4. In the plain style, the **い of the Vて-form いる** is also often dropped.  
    (Example: じしょ → じしょを かりている → じしょを かりてる)

5. **けど**  
    **けど** works in the same way as **が** and is often used in conversation.
