### _2. How to use quantifiers_

#### _1. Quantifiers (numbers + counter suffixes) _

-  Quantifiers (numbers + counter suffixes) are usually placed **after the noun + particle** that determines the type of quantifier.  
- However, this is **not always true** for **length of time**.
#### _2. Asking how many_

##### _1)_ **いくつ**
Used when asking the quantity of items counted with ひとつ、ふたつ、…、とお.

##### _2)_ **なん + counter suffix**
Used when asking the quantity of things that take a specific counter.

##### _3)_ **どのくらい**
Used to ask **how long** something takes (duration).

#### _3)_ **〜ぐらい**
Added after quantifiers to mean **“about.”**

---
### _3. 〜かい / Quantifier (time period) に ー<ruby>かい<rt>kai</rt></ruby> / <ruby>ー回<rt>かい</rt></ruby> V_

This expression indicates **frequency** (e.g., “three times per week”).

---
### _4. Quantifier だけ / N だけ_

- **だけ** means **“only.”**  
- It is added after quantifiers or nouns to indicate that there is no more or nothing else.

### _3. N₁ は N₂ より adj です_

This sentence pattern describes the quality and/or state of **N₁** in comparison with **N₂**.

---
### ***4. N₁ と N₂ と どちらが adj ですか***
#### …… N₁ / N₂ の ほうが adj です*

The interrogative **どちら** is used when comparing two items.  

---
### ***5. N₁ [の 中]（なか）で 何／どれ／だれ／いつ が いちばん adj ですか***

#### …… N₂ が いちばん adj です*  

- The particle **で** indicates a _range or category_.  
- This question pattern is used to ask the listener to choose, **from a group/category denoted by N₁** (a thing, place, person, time, etc.), the one that exhibits the **highest degree** of the quality described by the adjective.

The interrogative changes depending on what you're choosing:
- **何（なに）** → choosing a _thing_
- **どれ** → choosing _one item_ from several visible choices
- **だれ** → choosing a _person_    
- **いつ** → choosing a _time_

[Note] 
The particle が is attached to the interrogative even in an interrogative sentence asking about the subject of an adjective sentence (see Lesson 10).

## **Lesson 13**

---
### **1. Nがほしいです（欲しいです）**

ほしい is an い-adjective, and its object is marked by **が**.  

---
### **2. Vます-form たいです**

#### **a. Vます-form**

- The form a verb takes when used with ます (e.g. the **かい** of かいます) is called its **ます-form**.
#### **b. Vます-form たいです**

- The pattern **Vます-form たいです** is used to express the speaker’s desire to do something.  
- The object of 〜たい can be marked with **を** or **が**.  
- 〜たい inflects the same way as い-adjectives.

---
### **[Note 1]**

ほしいです and たいです can only be used when talking about what **the speaker or listener** wants.  
They cannot be used for **a third person**.
### **[Note 2]**

ほしいですか and Vます-form たいですか **cannot** be used when:
- offering something
- inviting someone

× コーヒーがほしいですか  
× コーヒーがのみたいですか

***Use instead:***
- ***コーヒーは　いかが　ですか*** (Important)
- ***コーヒーをのみませんか***

---
### **3. N(Place) へ Vます-form / N に 行きます・来ます・帰ります**

- This pattern marks the **purpose** of going, coming, or returning with ***に***.
- When the verb beforeに is Nします (e.g. かいものしますorべんきょうします) or Nをします (e.g. おはなみをします or つりをします), it is used in the form Nにいきます/きます/かえります。(Important)

**[Note]**  
When an event noun (festival, concert, etc.) comes before に, the purpose is interpreted as **seeing/listening/attending**.

---
### **4. どこか／なにか**

どこか = "somewhere / anywhere"  
なにか = "something / anything"

どこか → particle **へ**  
なにか → particle **を**  
(Both particles may be omitted.)

**[Note]**  ー relevant to the example in book ***but irrelevent to this  topic***
は may be added after a time word to make it the topic.

---
### **5. ご〜**

ご shows respect.

ごちゅうもん(注文)は？  
May I take your order?

## **Lesson 14**

---
### **1. Verb Groups**

#### **1) Group I Verbs**

The last sound of the ます-form is from the **い-column**.  
Examples:
- かきます (write)
- のみます (drink)
#### **2) Group II Verbs**

The last sound of the ます-form is mostly from the **え-column**:
- たべます (eat)
- みせます (show)

Some have an **い-column** sound:
- みます (see)

#### **3) Group III Verbs**

Includes:
- します
- Noun + します
- きます

---
### **2. Vて-form**

The verb form ending in **て / で** is called the **て-form**.  
How to form it depends on the verb group.

---
#### **1) Group I Verbs**

##### **(1)** ます-form ending in **い・ち・り**

→ drop ending → add **って**  
Examples:
- かいます → かって (buy)
- まちます → まって (wait)
- かえります → かえって (go home)

##### **(2)** ます-form ending in **み・び・に**

→ drop ending → add **んで**  
Examples:
- のみます → のんで (drink)
- よびます → よんで (call)
- しにます → しんで (die)
##### **(3)** ます-form ending in **き・ぎ**

→ drop ending → add
- **いて** (for き)
- **いで** (for ぎ)
Examples:
- かきます → かいて (write)
- いそぎます → いそいで (hurry)
**Exception:**
- いきます → いって
##### **(4)** ます-form ending in **し**

→ add **て**  
Example:
- かします → かして (lend)

---
#### **2) Group II Verbs**

Add **て** to the ます-form.  
Examples:
- たべます → たべて (eat)
- みせます → みせて (show)
- みます → みて (see)

---
#### **3) Group III Verbs**

Add **て** to the ます-form.  
Examples:
- きます → きて (come)
- します → して (do)
- さんぽします → さんぽして (go for a walk)

---
### **3. Vて-form ください**

Meaning: **Please do …**  
- This expression is used to tell, request, or invite the listener to do something.
- However, it is not a very polite way of asking someone to do something, so it is often used together with the expression すみませんが。_

---

## **4. Vて-form います**

This sentence pattern indicates that a certain action or motion is in progress.

---

## **5. Vます-form ましょうか**

_Shall I … ?_  
This expression is used when the speaker is offering to do something for the listener.

---

## **6. Nが V**

When describing a phenomenon by saying what was felt through one or more of the five senses (sight, sound, etc.) or when telling someone objectively about an event, the subject is marked with **が**.

---

## **7. すみませんが、......**

The **が** in expressions such as **すみませんが** and **しつれいですが** is not antithetical; it is a casual preamble used before addressing someone.
