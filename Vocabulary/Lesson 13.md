
| No. | Japanese | English Meaning | Mnemonics |

| 1 | あそびます | to play | “A so big playground!” → Imagine a kid shouting “Ah! So big!” when they see a playground — and then they play there. |
| 2 | およぎます | to swim | “Oh, Yogi’s swimming!” |
| 3 | むかえます | to go/come meet; to pick up, welcome | “Moo-car picks you up.” Picture a friendly cow driving a car to meet or pick you up — mukae → meet / pick up. |
| 4 | つかれます | to get tired | “Took-a-rest → tired.” Say it fast: took a rest ≈ tsukare → become tired. |
| 5 | けっこんします | to marry | “Cake + on → wedding!” At a wedding, someone drops cake on the floor — kekkon sounds like cake on → marry. |
| 6 | かいものします | to shop / go shopping | “Buy many (kaimono) things.” Kai (buy) + mono (things) → literally buy things → shopping. |
| 7 | こうえんを〜 | (in/through) the park | “Go in the park.” Kōen sounds like “Go-in” → remember park. |

| 10 | しょくじします | to have a meal / dine | “Shocks your G-I (stomach)!” Think of a meal so good or spicy that it shocks your G.I. tract — shokuji sounds like “shock G-I.” |
| 11 | ひろい | wide / spacious | “High road = wide road.” Imagine driving on a high road (hiroi) that’s super wide and open. “He roams freely.” A place so wide that he roams (hiroi) around without limits. |
