
| No. | Japanese | English Meaning | Mnemonics |
|-----|-----------|-----------------|------------|
| 1 | あけます | to open | “A key opens the door.” Ake sounds like “a key” → open. |
| 2 | しめます | to close, shut | “She made it closed.” Shime sounds like “she made” → close it. |
| 3 | いそぎます | to hurry | “I so go fast!” You shout “I so go!” while hurrying to school. |
| 4 | まちます | to wait | Much waiting for machi. |
| 5 | てつだいます | to help | “Let’s help with the test!” Tetsu like “test,” dai like “die” → help someone so they don’t “die” during the test. |
| 6 | よびます | to call (someone) | “Yo! Be here!” You call out to a friend: “Yo! Be here!” |
| 7 | はなします | to speak, talk | “Hannah talks a lot.” Hana sounds like “Hannah” → talk, speak. |
| 8 | とめます | to stop, park | “To my car—stop!” Tome sounds like “to my” → stop or park your car. |
| 9 | みせます | to show | “Let me see!” Mise sounds like “me say” → “Me say—look!” → show. |
| 10 | すわります | to sit | “Sit where?” → “Suwa right there.” Suwarimasu = sit down. |
| 11 | たちます | to stand | “Touch the sky—stand tall.” Tachi sounds like “touch” → stand up. |
| 12 | はいります（きっさてんに〜） | to enter (a café) | “Hi! I’m in.” You shout “Hi!” as you enter a room. |
| 13 | でます（きっさてんを〜） | to leave, go out (of a café) | “They must go out.” Demasu sounds like “they must” → leave, exit. |
| 14 | ふります（あめが〜） | to fall (rain, snow) | “Furry rain falls.” Imagine furry raindrops falling — furi = rain falls. |
| 15 | コピーします | to copy | “Copy she must.” Kopī shimasu literally means “do a copy.” |
| 16 | もちます | to hold, carry | “Mochi must hold shape.” Mochi is soft but it holds its shape → mochimasu = hold. |
| 17 | つけます | to turn on, attach, put on | “Stick it on!” Tsuke comes from 付ける (to attach). Imagine attaching or switching on a light → turn on. |
| 18 | けします | to turn off, erase | “Erase with a Ke-sure.” Think of an eraser named Keshor — every time you erase something, you say “Kesh it!” |
| 19 | つかいます | to use | “It’s okay, I’ll use it.” Tsukai sounds like “it’s okay.” Imagine saying “It’s okay, I’ll use it!” whenever something’s available. |
| 20 | エアコン | air conditioner | — |
| 21 | じゅうしょ | address | “Jew show me your address!” Imagine a jeweler saying, “Jew show me your address so I can deliver the gold!” |
| 22 | ちず | map | “Cheese map!” Picture a map with the treasure as cheese. |
| 23 | しお | salt | “She owes me salt.” Your friend forgot to return the salt, and you remind her, “She owe (shio) me salt!” |
| 24 | さとう | sugar | “Sat down for sugar.” You sit down to enjoy something sweet — satō = sugar. |
| 25 | もんだい | problem, question | “Monday = problem day.” Everyone hates Monday — it’s always full of problems! |
| 26 | こたえ | answer | “Got a answer → kotae.” You “got a” (kota) correct answer on your test. |
| 27 | よみかた | way of reading, pronunciation | “You read it your way.” Yomi = reading, kata = way → literally “reading way” → how to read or pronounce. |
| 28 | 〜かた | way of, method of | “Cutter shows the way.” Kata sounds like “cutter” — imagine a cutter showing you the way to do something. Example: つかいかた = “way to use” = how to use. |
| 29 | まっすぐ | straight, straight ahead | “March straight!” Masugu sounds like “march straight through.” |
| 30 | ゆっくり | slowly | “You cook curry slowly.” Yukkuri sounds like “you cook curry” → slowly. |
| 31 | すぐ | immediately, right away | “Soon = sugu.” They sound alike — both mean right away. “Do it sugu!” = Do it now. |
| 32 | また | again, see you | “Meet again — mata!” When you part ways, you say “Mata ne!” = “See you again!” |
| 33 | あとで | later | “After the day.” Ato = after, de = at/by → together “afterwards” → later. “I’ll do it ato de.” |
| 34 | もうすこし | a little more | “More-sko-shi = more a little.” Mō = more, sukoshi = little → literally “more a little” → a little more. “もうすこしください” = Please, a little more. |
