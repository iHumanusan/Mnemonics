
| No. | Japanese | English Meaning | Mnemonics |
|-----|-----------|-----------------|------------|
| 1 | うります | to sell | “U read me? I sell.” Uri sounds like “you read” — think of a salesman saying “You read me? I sell!” |
| 2 | しります | to know | “She remembers.” Shiri sounds like “she re–” → she remembers, so it means to know / to learn. |
| 3 | すみます | to live, reside | “Soon, me must live here.” Sumi → “soon me” → you live somewhere. |
| 4 | けんきゅうします | to research | “Can cue research!” You can cue your brain to study or research something. |
| 5 | しりょう | materials, data, documents | “She wrote materials.” Shiryō sounds like “she wrote,” and what she wrote are documents. |
| 6 | カタログ | catalogue, brochure | “Catalogue = kat-a-log.” Sounds exactly like “catalogue.” |
| 7 | じこくひょう | timetable, schedule | “Gee, clock shows schedule.” Ji = time, koku = interval, hyō = table → timetable (like for trains). |
| 8 | ふく | clothes | “You look good in fuku.” Fuku sounds like “frock” → clothes. |
| 9 | ソフト | software | “Soft → software.” English loanword — easy to remember. |
| 10 | せいひん | product, manufactured goods | “Say, fine product!” Seihin sounds like “say fine,” said when you see a fine product. |
| 11 | でんしじしょ | electronic dictionary | Denshi = electronic, jisho = dictionary → combine to form “electronic dictionary.” Think: “Dense dictionary” full of electrons. |
| 12 | けいざい | economy, economics | “Case I study — economics.” Keizai sounds like “case-eye,” and you study cases in economics class. |
| 13 | しやくしょ | city hall, municipal office | “She acts sure at city hall.” Shiyakusho sounds like “she acts sure” as she walks confidently into city hall. |
| 14 | こうこう | high school | “Go! Go! High school!” Kōkō = “go-go” → energetic high school students shouting it. |
| 15 | はいしゃ | dentist | “High-sharp tools — dentist!” Haisha sounds like “high-sharp” → those scary dentist tools. |
| 16 | どくしん | single, unmarried | “Don’t cushion — you’re single.” Picture a single person hugging a cushion alone → dokushin = single / unmarried. |
| 17 | すみません | excuse me, sorry, thank you | “So me’m a saint — I apologize.” Sumimasen sounds like “so me must say sorry” → excuse me / I’m sorry / thank you. |
