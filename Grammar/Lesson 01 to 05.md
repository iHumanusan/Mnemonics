
# **Lesson 1**

---
## **1. _N₁ は N₂です_**

### **Particle は**

- The particle は indicates that the noun before it (N₁) is the topic of the sentence.
### **です**

- Nouns used with です work as predicates.
- Besides indicating judgment or assertion, です also shows politeness towards the listener.
- です inflects when the sentence is negative or in the past tense.

---
## **2. _N₁はN₂じゃ(では)ありません_**

- **じゃ(では)ありません** is the negative form of です.
- **じゃありません** is often used in everyday conversation.
- **ではありません** is used in formal speech and writing.

---
## **3. _N₁はN₂ですか_**

### **Particle か**

- The particle か is used to express uncertainty, doubt, etc.    
- A question can be formed simply by adding か to the end of a sentence.
- A question usually ends with a rising intonation.
### **Questions asking whether a statement is correct**

- This type of question is formed simply by adding か, keeping the word order the same.
- Reply with はい if agreeing.
- Reply with いいえ if disagreeing.
### **Questions with interrogatives**

- The interrogative replaces the part being asked about.
- Word order does not change.
- か is added at the end.

---
## **4. _Nも_**

- **も** is used when the same thing applies as previously stated.

---
## **5. _N₁のN₂_**

- **の** is used to connect two nouns N₁ and N₂ when N₁ modifies N₂.

---
## **6. _~さん_**

- **さん** is added to the listener’s or third person’s name to show respect.    
- Never used for the speaker’s own name.
- **ちゃん** is used for small children.
- あなた is usually avoided if the speaker knows the listener’s name — instead, name + さん is used.
- **あなた** is used with someone very familiar (partner, spouse, etc.).

---
# **Lesson 2**

---
## **1. _これ / それ / あれ_**

- These are **demonstratives** and work as **nouns**.

---
## **2. _このN / そのN / あのN_**

- この、その、あの modify nouns.

---
## **3. _そうです_**

- Used to answer a yes/no question affirmatively.    
- はい、そうです is commonly used.

---
## **4. _〜か、〜か_**

- Used when asking the listener to choose between alternatives.
- Answers usually give the chosen option directly (not はい / いいえ).

---
## **5. _N₁のN₂_**

- Previously seen in Lesson 1.
- Additional uses:
    - N₁ explains what N₂ is about.
    - N₁ indicates ownership.    

---
## **6. _の substituting for a noun_**

- の can substitute for a previously mentioned noun.
- In N₁のN₂, N₂ can be omitted if already known.
- の substitutes for **things**, not people.    

---

## **7. _お~ (Honorific prefix)_**

- お is added to words to speak politely.    
- Added to things related to the listener/third party to show respect.

---
## **8. _そうですか_ （apdiya）**

- Used to acknowledge new information.
- Pronounced with falling intonation.

# **Lesson 3**

---
## **1. _ここ / そこ / あそこ / こちら / そちら / あちら_**

- こちら、そちら、あちら also indicate direction.
- They are politer versions of ここ、そこ、あそこ.

---
## **2. _N は place です_**

- States where a place, thing, or person is.

---
## **3. _どこ / どちら_**

- どこ = “Where?”    
- どちら = “Which direction?” or polite “Where?”
- Used to ask about affiliation (company, school, country, etc.) — なん cannot be used.
- どちら is more polite.

---
## **4. _N₁のN₂_**

- N₁ = country name
- N₂ = product name

---
## **5. _The こ / そ / あ / ど demonstrative system_**

|                            | こ series | そ series | あ series | ど series    |
| -------------------------- | -------- | -------- | -------- | ----------- |
| Thing                      | これ       | それ       | あれ       | どれ (L.16)   |
| Thing / Person             | この N     | その N     | あの N     | どの N (L.16) |
| Place                      | ここ       | そこ       | あそこ      | どこ          |
| Direction / Place (polite) | こちら      | そちら      | あちら      | どちら         |

# **Lesson 4**

---
## **1. _Time expression_**

### 今(いま)　一時(じ)　一分(ふん)　です


- 分 is read ふん after 2,5,7,9 and ぷん after 1,3,4,6,8,10.    
- 1,6,8,10 become いっ、ろっ、はっ、じゅっ/じっ before ぷん.

---
## **2. _Vます / Vません / Vました / Vませんでした_**

### **About Vます**

- **Vます works as a predicate.**
- Using **ます** shows politeness toward the listener.
- **Vます** is used when a sentence expresses:
    - Something **habitual**
    - A **general truth**
    - A **future behavior or event**
### **Forms**

**Non-past (present/future) / Past — Affirmative / Negative**

||Non-past|Past|
|---|---|---|
|**Affirmative**|おきます|おきました|
|**Negative**|おきません|おきませんでした|
### **Verb questions**

- Verb question sentences are formed by adding **か** at the end **without changing word order**.
- When using an interrogative, place it in the part of the sentence being asked about.
- When answering, **repeat the verb** from the question.
- **そうです** and **ちがいます** **cannot be used to answer verb questions**.

---

## **3. _N(time) に V_**

- に marks the time of occurrence.
    
- Not used with:  
    きょう、あした、あさって、きのう、おととい、けさ、こんばん、いま、まいあさ、まいばん、まいにち、せんしゅう、こんしゅう、らいしゅう、いつ、せんげつ、こんげつ、らいげつ、ことし、らいねん、きょねん…
    
- Optional with:  
    ようび、あさ、ひる、ばん、よる.
    

---

## **4. _N₁ から N₂ まで_**

- から = starting place/time
    
- まで = ending place/time
    
- They may be used separately or together.
    
- When the topic is a time expression, you can attach 〜から / 〜まで / 〜から〜まで + です.

---
## **5. _N₁ と N₂_**

- と connects two nouns.

---
## **6. _〜ね_**

- The particle **ね** is attached to the end of a sentence and is used to:
    - elicit agreement from the listener,
    - check that the listener has understood, or
    - emphasize something to the listener.

---
# **Lesson 5**

---
## **1. _N(place)へ 行きます / 来ます / 帰ります(かえります)_**

- When a verb indicates movement to a certain place, the particle **へ** is put after the place noun to show the direction of the move.
- The particle **へ** is pronounced **え**.

---
## **2. _どこ[へ]も 行きません / 行きませんでした_**

- When you want to deny everything covered by an interrogative, attach the particle **も** to the interrogative and put the verb in its **negative** form.

---
## **3. _N(vehicle)で 行きます / 来ます / 帰ります(かえります)_**

- The particle **で** indicates a **means or method**.
- Attach **で** after a noun representing a vehicle and use it with a movement verb to indicate the means of transport.
- When talking about **walking**, the expression **あるいて** is used — in this case, **で is not used**.

---
## **4. _N(person/animal) と V_**

- When talking about doing something **with a person or an animal**, the particle **と** is used.
- When doing something **alone**, the expression **ひとりで** is used — in this case, **と is not used**.

---
## **5. _いつ_**

- To ask about time, interrogatives with **なん**, such as なんじ、なんようび、なんがつなんにち, are used.
- The interrogative **いつ** (when) is also used to ask when something will happen or happened.
- **いつ does not take the particle に.**

---
## **6. _〜よ_**

- This particle is placed at the end of a sentence.
- It is used to:
    - emphasize information the listener does not know, or
    - show that the speaker is stating their judgement/assertion clearly.

---
## **7. _そうですね_**

- Used to express **sympathy** or **agreement** with what the other speaker has said.
- It is similar to **そうですか** (Lesson 2–8), but:
    - **そうですか** → acknowledges _new_ information
    - **そうですね** → shows _agreement or empathy_ with something the speaker already believed or knew
