# Lesson 21

---
## **1. Plain form と おもいます**
**I think that ……**

- The ideas or judgements expressed with **おもいます** are indicated by the particle **と**.
### **This sentence pattern is used in the following ways:**

### **1) When expressing conjecture**
- To express a negative conjecture, the clause before **と** is put in the **negative**.
### **2) When stating an opinion**
- When asking someone's opinion about something, the expression  **〜について どうおもいますか** is used, **without** putting **と** after **どう**.
### **Agreement with someone else's opinion**

ケータイはべんりですね。 Mobile phones are handy, aren't they?
		…………………わたしもそうおもいます。......Yes, I think so, too.

---
## **2. “Sentences” / Plain form と いいます**
**say ……**

- The particle **と** is used to report speech.
### **Two ways to report speech:**

#### **1) Direct quotation**
- Repeated word for word.  
- In writing, the words are placed inside **「 」**.
#### **2) Indirect quotation**
- The **plain form** is used before **と**.
- The tense of the quoted sentence is **not affected** by the tense of the main sentence.
- The particle **に** is used to indicate the person spoken to.

---
## **3.{[V／い–adj（plain form]／[な–adj（plain form）／N]（drop 〜だ）}＋ でしょう？**
**…… right?**

- This sentence form is used when seeking agreement or confirmation.  
- **でしょう** is spoken with rising intonation.
- The plain form is used before でしょう, but **without 〜だ** in the case of a **な–adj** or **noun**.

---
## **4. N₁（place）で N₂が あります**

- When N₂ is a ceremony or event (party, concert, festival, incident, disaster),  
**あります** means **“to take place / to be held / to occur.”**

---
## **5. N（occasion）で**

- The occasion at which an action takes place is marked by **で**.

---
## **6. Nでも V**

- Used when recommending, suggesting, or expressing hope for something.  
- **でも** gives an example **without limiting** it to a specific one.

---
## **7. V ない–form ないと……**

- This expression is created by omitting **いけません** from  
- **V ない–form ないと いけません**.

- It means roughly the same as  
- **V ない–form なければ なりません**.

# Lesson 22

---
## 1. **Noun Modification**

Lessons 2 and 8 explained how to modify nouns:
- ミラーさんのうち — Mr. Miller's house (Lesson 2)
- あたらしい うち — a new house (Lesson 8)
- きれいな うち — a beautiful house (Lesson 8)

- The modifying word or clause comes **before** the noun.
- This lesson explains **how to use a clause to modify a noun**.

---
### **1) Plain form before nouns**

Verbs, adjectives, and nouns in a clause that modifies a noun are in the **plain form**.
- For **な-adjectives** → **〜な**
- For **nouns** → **〜の**
### **Examples**

| Clause             | Noun | Meaning                           |
| ------------------ | ---- | --------------------------------- |
| きょうとへ 行く ひと        | →    | a person who goes to Kyoto        |
| きょうとへ 行かない ひと      | →    | a person who does not go to Kyoto |
| きょうとへ 行った ひと       | →    | a person who went to Kyoto        |
| きょうとへ 行かなかった ひと    | →    | a person who did not go to Kyoto  |
| せが たかくて、かみが くろい ひと | →    | a tall, black-haired person       |
| しんせつで、きれいな ひと      | →    | a beautiful, kind person          |
| ６５さい の ひと          | →    | a 65-year-old person              |

---
### **2) Noun-modifying clauses with different sentence patterns**

### **② これは ミラーさんが すんでいた うち です。**
This is the house where Mr. Miller used to live.
### **③ ミラーさんが すんでいた うちは ふるい です。**
The house where Mr. Miller used to live is old.
### **④ ミラーさんが すんでいた うちを かいました。**
I bought the house where Mr. Miller used to live.
### **⑤ わたしは ミラーさんが すんでいた うちが すきです。**
I like the house where Mr. Miller used to live.
### **⑥ ミラーさんが すんでいた うちに ねこが いました。**
There was a cat in the house where Mr. Miller used to live.
### **⑦ ミラーさんが すんでいた うちへ いった ことが あります。**
I've been to the house where Mr. Miller used to live.

---
### **3) Subject of a noun-modifying clause**

The **subject** of a clause modifying a noun is marked by **が** (not は).

Example:

- ミラーさんが すんでいた うち  
    → "the house **where Mr. Miller** used to live"
    

---
## **2. V-dictionary form + じかん / やくそく / ようじ**

When talking about:
- the **time required** to do something, or
- describing an **appointment, promise, errand**, etc.,
- the verb is put in the **dictionary form** before the noun じかん / やくそく / ようじ, etc.

---
# **3. Vます-form + ましょうか**
**Shall we ~ ?**

- In Lesson 14: used when offering to do something _for the listener_.    
- In this lesson: used when suggesting **doing something together**.
    
Example:
- いっしょに 行きましょうか。  
    → Shall we go together?

# **Lesson 23**

---
# **1. [V-dictionary form / Vない-form / い-adj（〜い） / な-adj な / N の] ＋ とき、〜（main clause）**
**When ~ …**

- **とき** connects two sentences and expresses the **time or occasion** when the state or action in the main clause occurs.    
- The form used before **とき** is the **same form used when modifying a noun**.
- The **tense** of the clause before とき is **independent** of the tense in the main clause.

---
# **2. [V-dictionary form / Vた-form] ＋ とき、〜（main clause）**
**When ~ …**

### Meaning difference:

| Form                     | Meaning                                             |
| ------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------- |
| **V-dictionary form とき** | Main clause happens **before** the とき-clause action |
| **Vた-form とき**           | Main clause happens **after** the とき-clause action  |

### Examples:

**⑧ パリへ 行く とき、かばんを 買いました。**  
I bought a bag when going to Paris.  
→ Bag was bought **before arriving** in Paris (on the way).

**⑨ パリへ 行った とき、かばんを 買いました。**  
I bought a bag when I went to Paris.  
→ Bag was bought **after arriving** in Paris (in Paris).

---
# **3. V-dictionary form ＋ と、〜（main clause）**
**If… then…**

Used to show that:
- If a certain **action / situation / natural phenomenon** occurs (before と),
- another action or situation **will inevitably** follow (after と).

It expresses a **natural result**, **automatic consequence**, or **habitual reaction**.

---
# **4. N が adj**

As explained in Lesson 14:
- **が** is used for describing things perceived with the **five senses**,  
    or when **objectively reporting** an event.
- が can be used not only with verbs, but also with **adjective** sentences.

Example patterns:
- こえが ちいさいです。 — The voice is soft.
- あめが おおいです。 — There is a lot of rain.

---
# **5. N を (motion verb)**

- Used with **motion verbs** to indicate the **place** that someone or something **passes through**.

Verbs: さんぽします、わたります、あるきます etc.

Examples:
- こうえん を さんぽします。 — Walk through the park.
- みち を わたります。 — Cross the street.
- はし を あるきます。 — Walk across the bridge.

# **Lesson 24**

---
# **1. くれます**

- The verb **あげます (give)** cannot be used when talking about **someone else giving something to the speaker** or **to the speaker’s family**.
- In those cases, **くれます** is used.

---
# **2. Vて-form ＋ あげます / もらいます / くれます**

あげます、もらいます、くれます describe **giving and receiving things**.  
But:
- **〜てあげます**
- **〜てもらいます**
- **〜てくれます**

describe **actions** that give some **benefit** or **favor** to someone.

---
## **1) Vて-form あげます**
- Used when the **doer of the action** is the subject.
- Shows that the action gives some **benefit** to the recipient.
- Can sound **patronizing** if used when the recipient is of **higher status**.
- In such cases, use **Vます-form＋ましょうか** (Lesson 14-5) instead.

---
## **2) Vて-form もらいます**
- Shows that the **receiver of the action** (the subject) **benefits** from the action.    
- When the subject is **わたし**, it is usually **omitted**.

---
## **3) Vて-form くれます**
- The **doer** of the action is the subject.
- Shows that the **receiver** (marked by に) benefits from the action.
- When the receiver is **わたし**, **に** is usually omitted.

---
### **Note:**

In **〜てあげます** and **〜てくれます** sentences, the particle marking the **beneficiary** is the **same** as in sentences **without** those forms.

わたし***に*** りょこうの しゃしんを みせます。
わたし***に*** りょこうの しゃしんを みせてくれます。
He shows me the photos taken during the travel.

---

# **3. N₁ は N₂ が V**

Example reference:  
さとうさんが このワインを くれました。

When responding, the **topic** becomes the **object** (このワインを).  
Since both speaker and listener already understand this, it is **omitted**:
   → （このワインは） さとうさんが くれました。
- The **subject** is **さとうさん**, so it is marked by **が**.

# Lesson 25

---

# **1. Plain past form ＋ ら、〜 (main clause)**
**If ……**

- Attaching **ら** to the **plain past form** of verbs, adjectives, or nouns makes the clause **conditional**.  
- The clause that follows it (the **main clause**) describes what will happen **if the conditional clause were true**.  
-  The speaker’s **opinion, wishes, invitations, requests**, etc., can be expressed in the main clause.

---
## **[Note]**

Expressions of **wish, desire, invitation, request**, etc. **cannot** be used in a main clause following **〜と**.

× **時間が あると、**
- コンサートに 行きます。 (intention)    
- コンサートに 行きたいです。 (wish)
- コンサートに 行きませんか。 (invitation)
- ちょっと 手伝って ください。 (request)

---
# **2. V た-form ＋ ら、〜 (main clause)**
**When…… / After…… / Once……**

- This pattern expresses that **a certain action will be done** or **a certain situation will arise** once a specific action or event is completed.  
- The speaker is certain it will happen.

---

# **3. [Vて-form / Vない-form なくて / い-adj (no い → 〜くて) / な-adj (no な → 〜で) / Nで] ＋ も、〜 (main clause)**

### **Even if ……**

- This expression is used to present **contrasting conditions**.  
- The clause that follows **〜ても** (the **main clause**) describes something that **will still happen**, even though normally it **would not be expected** if the condition in the first clause were true.

---
# **4. もし**

- **もし** is used together with **〜たら** to show clearly that the following clause is **conditional**.  
- It **intensifies** or **emphasizes** the speaker’s sense of “if.”

---
# **5. Subject of a subordinate clause**

Lesson 16–2 explained that the subject of a clause with **〜てから** is marked by **が**.

In the same way, for subordinate clauses containing:
- **〜とき**
- **〜と**
- **〜まえに**
- **〜たら**
- **〜ても**

→ The **subject** of the subordinate clause is also marked by **が**.
