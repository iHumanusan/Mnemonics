# **1. 〜んです**

### **Forms**

| Type                             | Plain form rule    | + んです |
| -------------------------------- | ------------------ | ----- |
| Verb / い-adjective / な-adjective | plain form         | んです   |
| Noun                             | drop だ → add **な** | んです   |

---
### **Meaning**
〜んです is used to **strongly explain causes, reasons, or background**.
#### BUT
- 〜んです → spoken only    
- 〜のです → written only

---
## **1) 〜んですか**

Used in the following situations:
1. When the speaker **guesses** the reason/cause based on what they saw or heard and asks for confirmation.
2. When the speaker asks for information what he has seen or heard
3. When the speaker wants the listener to **explain the reason** for something.
4. When asking for an explanation of the situation.
	① **どうしたんですか。**  
	What's the matter?

**Note:**  
〜んですか can contain **surprise, suspicion, or strong curiosity**. Incorrect or excessive use may sound rude.

---
## **2) 〜んです**

Used in the following cases:
1. When _answering_ the questions of type (3) or (4) above, states the reason or cause.
2. When the speaker adds a reason/explanation to what they have stated.

**Note:**  
〜んです is **not used for simple factual statements**.

Correct:  
○ わたしはマイク・ミラーです。  
Incorrect:  
× わたしはマイク・ミラーなんです。

---
## **3) 〜んですが、〜**

- Used to **introduce a topic** and is followed by a request, invitation, or seeking permission.  
- It softens the tone and shows **hesitation or reservation**.
- Often, the clause after 〜んですが is omitted when obvious.

**日本語で手紙を書いたんですが、ちょっと見ていただけませんか。**  
I've written a letter in Japanese. Could you please check it for me?

**お湯が出ないんですが………………。**  
There's no hot water.

---
# **2. Vて-form いただけませんか**
Meaning: **Would you please do me the favor of ~ing?**

- A **politer** request than 〜てください.

---
# **3. Interrogative + Vた-form らいいですか**

|Question word (What/When/Where/Which/How/Who)|Meaning|
|---|---|
|+ Vたらいいですか|What shall I do? / What is good to do?|

- Used when asking for **advice or instructions**.

---
# **4. は used with objects**

| Pattern                                        | Meaning examples                       |
| ---------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| N (object) は すきです／きらいです / じょうずです / へたです / あります | like/dislike, be good at/bad at, have… |

From Book I (Lessons 10, 17):
- Objects marked with **を** can become topics using **は**.
- Objects marked with **が** can also be highlighted with **は**.

## 1. Potential Verbs

### How to make potential verbs

**Group I**

| Verb (ます-form) | Potential (polite) | Potential (plain) |
| -------------- | ------------------ | ----------------- |
| かきます           | かけます               | かける               |
| かいます           | かえます               | かえる               |

**Group II**

| Verb | Potential (polite) | Potential (plain) |
| ---- | ------------------ | ----------------- |
| たべます | たべられます             | たべられる             |

**Group III**

| Verb | Potential (polite) | Potential (plain) |
| ---- | ------------------ | ----------------- |
| します  | できます               | できる               |
| きます  | こられます              | こられる              |
|      |                    |                   |

**Notes:**

- All potential verbs are treated as **Group II** for conjugation (dictionary form, ない-form, て-form, etc.).  
    Example: かける → かけ(ない) → かけて
- わかる already contains the meaning of possibility, so it does **not** become わかれる.


---
## 2. Potential Verb Sentences

### (1) Meaning and particles

- Potential verbs express **states**, not actions.
- Transitive verbs normally take **を**, but when used in potential form, the object is usually marked by **が**.

Examples:
1. わたしは日本語を話します。
2. わたしは日本語が話せます。
    - I speak Japanese.
    - I can speak Japanese.
    - Only を changes.
3. 一人で病院へ行けますか。  
    Can you go to the hospital alone?
4. 田中さんに会えませんでした。  
    I could not see Mr. Tanaka.
    - No change in へ and に
### (2) Two uses of potential form

- Ability    
- Possibility depending on a situation

---
## 3. 見えます and 聞こえます

- Potential of みます = **みられます**

- Potential of ききます = **きけます**  
    They mean “can see intentionally” or “can listen intentionally.”

- **見えます** = something **comes into sight** (not intentional)
- **聞こえます** = sound **reaches the ears** (not intentional)

- Objects become subjects and take **が**.
- Even though their meanings are “unintentional perception,” grammatically they behave like regular ichidan verbs.
- **見える** and **聞こえる** **cannot** be converted into an additional potential form.
- 見える and 聞こえる already contain the meaning of “potential”

---
## 4. できます

Meaning here:  
“come into being,” “come up,” “be completed,” “be finished,” “be made,” etc.

Examples:
1. 駅の前に大きいスーパーができました。  
    A big supermarket has been completed in front of the station.
2. (とけい)時計の(しゅり)修理はいつできますか。  
    When can you fix this watch?

---
## 5. は

### (1) Particles + は
- では / には / へは / からは / までは etc.
- は highlights a noun as a topic.
- When が or を would follow the noun, they are replaced by は.
- With other particles, は is added **after** them.
### (2) は for contrast

Examples:
- きのうは山が見えましたが、きょうは見えません。  
    Yesterday we could see mountains, but not today.
- ワインは飲みますが、ビールは飲みません。  
    I drink wine, but not beer.
- 京都へは行きますが、(おおさか)大阪へは行きません。  
    I will go to Kyoto, but not to Osaka.

---
## ６. も

- Like は, も replaces **を** or **が**, but follows other particles.    
- With へも, へ can be omitted.

Example:

- わたしの(へや)部屋から(うみ)海が見えます。弟の部屋からも見えます。  
    The sea can be seen from my room, and also from my brother’s room.

---
### Additional Notes on Particles
- から (kara) and まで (made) are fundamental particles.
- 助詞 (joshi) / て、に、を、は、etc = particles that follow nouns/verbs to show grammatical functions.

**Key Particles**
- は – topic
- が – subject
- を – object
- の – possession
- に – location/time
- で – location of action, means
- も – also/too
- と – and/quotation
- か – question marker
- から – from/since/because
- まで – to/until

---
## 8. しか

- Attached to nouns or quantifiers.
- Must be used with **negative predicates**.
- Structure: **N + しか + NEGATIVE VERB**
- Means “only,” with a **limiting** nuance.
- Replaces が or を, follows other particles.
- しか = negative nuance
- だけ = positive nuance
- しか is a **limiting particle**, and だけ is too.

# Lesson 28

---
## 1. V₁ます-form ながら V₂

- **Meaning:** One person does two different actions (V₁ and V₂) **at the same time**. The action denoted by V₂ is **more emphasized**.
- **Examples:**

1. (おんがく)音楽を聞きながら(しょくじ)食事します。
    _I listen to music while eating._
2. (はたら)働きながら日本語を(べんきょう)勉強しています。
    _I'm working and studying Japanese._
- This pattern is also used when the two actions take place over a **period of time**.

---
## 2. Vて-form います

- Expresses an **individual’s customary action**.
- **Past customary action:** use **Vて-form いました**.
- **Examples:**
1. (まいあさ)毎朝ジョギングをしています。  
    _I jog every morning._
2. 子どものとき、(まいげん)毎晩8時に(ね)寝ていました。  
    _I used to go to bed at eight every evening when I was a child._

---
## 3. Plain form し、

### Usage 1: Listing merits or viewpoints
- Sentences can be linked using **し** to present multiple points about a subject.
- **Example:**  
    ワット先生はねっしんだし、まじめだし、けいけんもあります。  
    _Professor Watt is earnest and diligent and has experience._
### Usage 2: Stating multiple causes or reasons
- Implies “furthermore” or “on top of that.”
- **Example:**  
    駅から(ちかい)近いし、車でも来られるし、この店はとても(べんり)便利です。
    _This shop is very convenient. It's near the station, and you can also come here by car._
- Sometimes the conclusion is **omitted** when understood from context:  
    （むすこ)息子に(えいご)英語を(おし)教えていただけませんか。
		･･･うーん、しゅっちょうも多いし、もうすぐ日本語の( しけん )試験もあるし･･････。  (usage of も also indicates that the speaker implies the existence of other reasons to emphasize his/her viewpoint. )
			Could you teach English to my son?
		    _Sorry. I often go on business trips, and I have to take a Japanese exam soon._

- Even if only one reason is explicitly stated, **other reasons are implied** by **し**.
- Note this is not true of ~から.
- **Example:**  
    (いろ)色もきれいだし、この(くつ)靴を買います。
    _Because the color is beautiful (and for other reasons), I’ll buy this pair of shoes._

---
## 4. それに

- Used to **add another similar fact or situation**.
- **Example:**  
    どうして さくら大学を(えら)選んだんですか。
	 ･･･さくら大学は、父が(で)出た大学だし、いい先生も多いし、それに(いえ)家から(ちか)近いですから。
    _Why did you choose Sakura University?  
    Because it’s my father’s alma mater, there are many good teachers, and besides, it’s near my house._

---
## 5. それで

- **Conjunction** used to show that the previous statement is the **reason or cause** for the next.
- **Example:**  
    このレストランは(ねだん)値段も(やす)安いし、おいしいんです。
	･･･それでひとが多いんですね。
	    _The food is inexpensive and tasty in this restaurant.  
	    ...And that’s why it’s crowded._

---
## 6. Particle に for destination

- The particle **に** indicates a person’s **destination**, as opposed to **へ**, which shows **direction**.
- Verbs like **いきます、きます、かえります、しゅっちょうします** can use either **place へ** or **place に**.
- **Example:**  
    よくこの(きっさてん)喫茶店に来るんですか  
    _Do you come to this café often?_
